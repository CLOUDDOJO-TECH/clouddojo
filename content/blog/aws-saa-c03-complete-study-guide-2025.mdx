## Exam Overview

**Exam Code**: SAA-C03
**Duration**: 130 minutes
**Number of Questions**: 65 questions (multiple choice and multiple response)
**Passing Score**: 720 out of 1000
**Cost**: $150 USD
**Validity**: 3 years
**Format**: Pearson VUE testing center or online proctored

**Question Types:**
- Multiple choice: One correct answer out of four options
- Multiple response: Two or more correct answers out of five or more options

## Exam Domains & Topics

The SAA-C03 exam is divided into four domains:

### Domain 1: Design Secure Architectures (30%)

**Key Topics:**
- Secure application tiers
- Secure data at rest and in transit
- Define comprehensive security measures
- Design network infrastructure

**Critical Services:**
- IAM (Users, Groups, Roles, Policies)
- VPC (Subnets, Route Tables, NACLs, Security Groups)
- AWS Organizations & SCP
- KMS & CloudHSM
- Secrets Manager & Systems Manager Parameter Store
- CloudTrail & Config
- GuardDuty, Macie, Inspector

### Domain 2: Design Resilient Architectures (26%)

**Key Topics:**
- Design scalable and loosely coupled architectures
- Design highly available and fault-tolerant architectures
- Storage solutions for different workloads

**Critical Services:**
- Multi-AZ deployments
- Auto Scaling Groups
- Elastic Load Balancers (ALB, NLB, GWLB)
- S3 (storage classes, lifecycle policies, versioning)
- EBS vs EFS vs FSx
- RDS Multi-AZ, Aurora, DynamoDB
- Route 53 (routing policies, health checks)
- CloudFront & Global Accelerator

### Domain 3: Design High-Performing Architectures (24%)

**Key Topics:**
- Determine high-performing storage solutions
- Design high-performing and elastic compute solutions
- Determine high-performing database solutions
- Determine high-performing networking solutions

**Critical Services:**
- EC2 instance types & families
- Lambda & serverless patterns
- ECS, EKS, Fargate
- ElastiCache (Redis, Memcached)
- RDS Read Replicas
- DynamoDB DAX
- VPC Peering, Transit Gateway
- Direct Connect, VPN

### Domain 4: Design Cost-Optimized Architectures (20%)

**Key Topics:**
- Design cost-optimized storage solutions
- Design cost-optimized compute solutions

**Critical Services:**
- S3 Intelligent-Tiering, Glacier
- EC2 Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, Spot Instances
- Cost Explorer, Budgets, Cost Allocation Tags
- Trusted Advisor
- AWS Compute Optimizer
- Lambda cost optimization

- Days 19-21: Practice exams & review weak areas

**Week 4: Final Prep**
- Days 22-25: Full-length practice exams
- Days 26-28: Review flagged questions
- Day 29: Rest day

### 60-Day Standard Plan (Part-Time Study)

Study 2-3 hours per day, covering the same topics but at a more relaxed pace. Dedicate weekends to hands-on labs and practice exams.

### 90-Day Comprehensive Plan (Beginners)

Ideal for those new to AWS. Spend the first month on AWS fundamentals, then follow the 60-day plan.

## Exam Day Tips

### Before the Exam

- ‚úÖ Schedule exam 2-4 weeks in advance
- ‚úÖ Review exam policies and requirements
- ‚úÖ Test your equipment if taking online
- ‚úÖ Get 8 hours of sleep the night before
- ‚úÖ Eat a good breakfast
- ‚úÖ Arrive early (15-30 minutes for test center)

### During the Exam

**Time Management:**
- You have 130 minutes for 65 questions = 2 minutes per question
- Don't spend more than 2 minutes on any question initially
- Flag difficult questions and return later
- Aim to complete first pass in 90 minutes

**Strategy:**
- Read questions carefully (watch for "NOT" or "EXCEPT")
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
- Look for key phrases: "most cost-effective," "most secure," "least operational overhead"
- When in doubt, choose AWS managed services over self-managed
- Don't change answers unless you're confident you misread

**Common Exam Tricks:**
- Both answers might work, but one is "better" based on the scenario
- Watch for multi-AZ vs multi-region requirements
- Cost optimization often favors serverless/managed services
- Security questions often have multiple correct layers
